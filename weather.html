WEATHER IOT

Tasked with creating an I.o.T. device in our final year at Humber College, my partner for this assignment Tyler Nicol and I had decided to create a personal weather device and app. Using the BME280 sensor and an Arduino we used Node.js to pull data every 30 minutes from the sensor and then store it on MongoDB. From there the data gets called in a few different ways. The first way is to show you basic information, meaning what it is like within the past 30 minutes. Secondly, if you tap on a section for example temperature it will display a chart of the past 5 temperature readings.

Another feature of this assignment was to predict any rainfall using the barometer in the sensor. We would take those 5 past readings and see if the pressure has fallen or dropped. Based on that data the app displays whether it will rain, thunderstorm, or be clear skies.
